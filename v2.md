## 1. The Thesis

**Days Until is building the temporal intelligence layer** — the AI-native platform that makes time feel real, prepares you for what's coming, and helps you understand your own life's rhythm.

Every tool ever built to manage time has been built around *logistics* — when is the meeting, what's the deadline, did you complete the task. None of them have been built around the *emotional weight* of time. The fact that your father's 70th birthday is in 23 days. That your visa expires in 47 days. That you've been sober for 614 days. That your startup's runway ends in 91 days.

These are not calendar events. These are the moments that define a life. And nobody has built great software for them.

The market opportunity is hiding in plain sight: **5 billion people on earth have things they're counting down to**. Nobody owns that category. We will.

## 2. The Problem — Deep Research

### The Time Awareness Gap

Studies show that humans are systematically bad at perceiving future time. This is called **time discounting** — events far in the future feel abstract, unreal, and fail to motivate action until they're dangerously close. The psychological literature calls this *temporal myopia*.

The consequence:
- People miss major life deadlines (visa renewals, insurance enrollments, passport expirations) because they feel abstract until they're urgent
- Teams miss product launches because nobody felt the deadline was real until 3 days before
- People fail to prepare for important moments (job interviews, medical appointments, important conversations) because "there's still time"
- Relationships suffer because anniversaries, birthdays, and milestones go unacknowledged — not from lack of care, but lack of awareness

### The Existing Tool Failure

**Google Calendar** treats every event equally — your dentist appointment gets the same 15-minute reminder as your wedding anniversary. It has no concept of emotional significance, preparation requirements, or life context. It's a scheduling grid, not a life tool.

**Apple Reminders / Todoist** are task managers. They're excellent at "buy milk." They're terrible at "my daughter's first birthday is in 14 days — what should I be doing right now?"

**Notion / Confluence** let you write about upcoming milestones, but they don't proactively surface them, don't connect them to your calendar, and don't give you any sense of urgency.

**Countdown apps** (existing) are either toy widgets (big number, pretty background) or niche utilities (wedding countdown apps). None have AI. None have social. None have team features. None have a business model beyond a one-time purchase.

### The Scale of the Problem

- **$650B** — global project management software market (just the B2B slice of deadline management)
- **4.3B** — smartphone users who get zero intelligent time awareness
- **67%** — of people report feeling regularly surprised by how quickly time passed before a major event (Gallup)
- **$400B** — estimated cost of missed deadlines to US businesses annually (PMI)
- **43%** — of Gen Z and Millennial users say they want AI to help them plan and prepare for life events (Morning Consult, 2024)

### The Jobs To Be Done

Breaking down what people actually need when they care about time:

1. **"I need to know how long I have"** — the basic countdown
2. **"I need to not forget"** — reminders at the right time
3. **"I need to prepare"** — what do I actually need to do before this?
4. **"I want to share this"** — make others aware, celebrate together
5. **"I want to track progress"** — am I on track? Has my team shipped in time?
6. **"I want to reflect"** — what happened? What did I learn? What's the pattern?

No single product satisfies all six. Days Until will.

## 3. The Insight

The core insight that makes this a venture-scale company:

**Time is the universal interface.** Every human, in every culture, in every industry, organizes their life around time. But we've never had an AI that actually *understands* your relationship with time — not just when things happen, but what they mean to you, how you prepare, how you feel about them, and what patterns define your temporal life.

**The second insight:** The moment you make time social, it becomes a distribution engine. Every shared countdown, every public wedding page, every team deadline board is a viral marketing touchpoint. Calendly grew to $3B by making scheduling links shareable. Days Until will grow by making *moments* shareable.

**The third insight:** Enterprise willingness to pay for deadline intelligence is enormous and underserved. The productivity tools market ($57B) is captured by task management (Asana, Linear, Jira). The adjacent market — *time-to-milestone intelligence* — has no clear winner. This is where Days Until wins in B2B.

## 5. Product — Layer by Layer

### Layer 1: Personal Intelligence — V2 Features

#### 1.1 AI Event Understanding
When a user adds any event, the AI silently classifies it:
- **Event type** → `birthday`, `travel`, `health`, `finance`, `career`, `personal`, `deadline`, `celebration`, `anniversary`, `recurring_life_admin`
- **Emotional weight** → `high` / `medium` / `low` (inferred from name + type)
- **Preparation complexity** → `none` / `simple` / `moderate` / `complex`

This classification powers everything else: prep checklists, reminder timing, notification tone, dashboard grouping.

**Implementation:** Fine-tuned classification model on top of an existing LLM. Run at event creation time. Result stored with the event document.

#### 1.2 AI Prep Checklists
For events with `preparation_complexity: moderate+`, the AI generates a checklist that appears as the event approaches.

**Timeline:** Checklist appears when `days_until ≤ preparation_window` (varies by event type).

**Examples:**

`event: "Job interview at Google", days_until: 14`
→ AI generates:
- Research the team and interviewer on LinkedIn
- Review the job description line by line
- Prepare STAR stories for behavioral questions
- Do 3 mock system design sessions
- Research Google's recent product launches
- Prepare 5 questions to ask them
- Plan your outfit and logistics 2 days before
- Get 8 hours of sleep the night before

`event: "Flight to Tokyo", days_until: 10`
→ AI generates:
- Check passport expiration (must be valid 6+ months beyond return)
- Apply for travel insurance
- Notify bank of international travel
- Download offline maps
- Research local SIM card or eSIM options
- Pack adapters (Japan uses Type A, 100V)
- Check baggage allowance for your ticket class
- Download translation app

`event: "Annual performance review", days_until: 21`
→ AI generates:
- Document all major projects completed this year
- Collect quantitative impact metrics
- List skills developed
- Prepare salary negotiation target and BATNA
- Schedule 1:1s with advocates who can provide context
- Write first draft of self-evaluation

**UX:** Checklist lives inside the event card (expandable). Each item is checkable. Progress tracked. Percentage shown on card face ("7 of 12 prep steps done").

**AI model:** GPT-4o or Claude Sonnet via API, with heavy prompt engineering and caching. Cost per checklist generation: ~$0.002. Only generated once per event, cached forever.

#### 1.3 Smart Reminder System
Not a single "15 minutes before" reminder. A cascading, context-aware notification system.

**Reminder architecture:**

```
Event: "Passport Renewal" (deadline, preparation_complexity: complex)

T-90 days: "Your passport expires in 90 days. Government processing times are currently 6-8 weeks — now is a good time to start."
T-60 days: "60 days until passport expiration. Have you submitted your renewal? [Yes / Remind me in 1 week]"
T-45 days: "45 days — if you haven't submitted, consider expedited processing ($60 extra, 2-3 weeks)."
T-30 days: "30 days. Expedited processing is now essential. Here's the direct link."
T-14 days: "⚠️ 14 days. Contact the passport agency directly for emergency appointment."
T-7 days: "🚨 7 days. Emergency appointment or consider trip postponement."
```

```
Event: "Mom's Birthday" (birthday, emotional_weight: high)

T-14 days: "Your mom's birthday is in 2 weeks. Good time to order a gift with standard shipping."
T-7 days: "1 week until your mom's birthday. Have you planned how you're celebrating?"
T-3 days: "3 days! If ordering online, select 2-day shipping today."
T-1 day: "Tomorrow is your mom's birthday 🎂"
Day-of: "Happy birthday to your mom! Don't forget to call."
T+1 day: "Hope yesterday was special. Did you want to journal anything about it?"
```

Reminder timing is personalized over time — if a user consistently dismisses reminders at T-14 but acts on T-7, the system learns and adjusts.

**Notification channels:**
- Push notifications (PWA service worker)
- Email digests (weekly "What's coming up" summary)
- SMS (premium tier — via Twilio)
- Slack DM (team tier integration)

#### 1.4 Count-Up / Days Since Mode
A fundamentally different emotional experience — tracking elapsed time, not remaining time.

**Use cases:**
- Sobriety / clean days
- Days since a loss (grief tracking)
- Days into a new job, relationship, city
- Streak tracking (days of working out, meditating, not smoking)
- Time since a medical procedure / recovery progress
- Days since launching your company

**UX specifics:**
- Count-up events display differently — large number up front, "days since [date]"
- Milestone celebrations at key numbers: 7, 14, 30, 60, 90, 100, 180, 365, 500, 730, 1000, 1500, 2000
- Milestone notification: "You've been sober for 1 year. That's 365 days of showing up for yourself."
- Optional private mode — count-up events can be hidden from social features entirely
- Optional milestone sharing — "Share your 100-day milestone" → generates beautiful card image for social media

**Mental health consideration:** Recovery and grief tracking requires careful, non-gamified UX. No streaks that "break." No pressure language. Milestone celebrations are warm, not competitive.

#### 1.5 Life Patterns Dashboard
After 90+ days of usage, AI begins surfacing patterns about the user's temporal life.

**Insights examples:**
- "You've added 12 health-related events this year but only 3 last year. Your health awareness is growing."
- "Every year around November, you add a lot of social events. Your social calendar tends to peak in fall."
- "You have 4 financial deadlines in the next 60 days — more than usual. This might be a high-stress period."
- "You haven't taken a trip in 8 months. Your last vacation was [date]."
- "You tend to add prep tasks for events 2 weeks before. You're a planner."
- "3 of your last 5 work deadlines were marked complete on the last day. Cutting it close."

**Privacy:** All pattern analysis is local/on-device or happens in an isolated user context. Never used for advertising. Explicitly opt-in.

#### 1.6 Timezone-Aware Events
Every event stores:
- `timezone: "America/Los_Angeles"`
- `countdown_logic: "deadline"` (counts to midnight in user's timezone) or `"moment"` (counts to exact time)

When collaborators in different timezones view a shared event, they see the countdown in their own timezone with a note: "Ends at 11:59 PM PST / 2:59 AM EST"

#### 1.7 Recurrence — Full Feature Spec
Current recurrence is too simple. V2:

**Recurrence patterns:**
- Every N days / weeks / months / years
- Every [weekday] (e.g., every Tuesday)
- Every [Nth weekday of month] (e.g., first Monday of every month)
- Every [month-day] (e.g., every March 15)
- Twice weekly, twice monthly
- Custom: every 3 weeks

**Recurrence modifiers:**
- End after N occurrences
- End on specific date
- Skip individual occurrence without affecting series
- "Edit this occurrence only" vs "Edit all future occurrences" (standard calendar pattern)

**Skip occurrence UX:** Swipe left on a recurring event → "Skip this occurrence" → card disappears, reappears for next occurrence.

#### 1.8 ICS Export
Export any event or the entire event list as a `.ics` file.

- Individual event: "Add to Calendar" button → downloads `.ics` → opens in Apple/Google Calendar
- Bulk export: Settings → Export → "Export as .ics" downloads all events
- Subscribed calendar feed: Generate a URL that any calendar app can subscribe to (auto-updates when events change). This is the most powerful — changes in Days Until instantly appear in Google Calendar.

**Subscribed calendar feed implementation:**
- Unique URL per user: `https://daysuntil.app/cal/{user_token}.ics`
- Served as valid iCalendar format
- Protected by user-specific token (not UID)
- Refreshed every 30 minutes by subscribing calendar apps
- User can rotate the token if they want to revoke access

#### 1.9 Event Categories & Tags
Beyond color, events get structured categorization:

**System categories:** Life Admin, Health, Career, Finance, Travel, Family, Social, Personal, Work, Education

**Custom tags:** User-defined. `#wedding-planning`, `#q4-goals`, `#house-hunt`

**Filter by category/tag** in the event list. Category icons shown on cards. Dashboard view grouped by category.

#### 1.10 Pinned Events
Up to 5 events can be pinned to the top of the list regardless of date order. Use case: runway countdown, visa expiration, wedding date — things that should always be visible.

**Subscriber notifications:**
When someone subscribes to a countdown page:
- Confirmation email/text
- Reminders at T-7, T-1, T-0 (customizable by event creator)
- Day-of celebration message

**Viral loop:** Every subscriber is a potential new user. After the event, they get: "Maya's Wedding was amazing. Create your own countdown at Days Until."

#### 2.2 Collaborative Events
Two or more users co-own an event. Both see it in their personal timeline. Both can edit it. Both receive reminders.

**Use cases:**
- Couple tracking their anniversary
- Two friends counting down to a trip
- Parent and child tracking graduation
- Business partners tracking a funding deadline

**Invitation flow:** Event owner → "Invite collaborator" → enters email → recipient gets magic link → accepts → event appears in their timeline.

**Conflict resolution:** Last-write-wins for simple fields. For recurring events with skipped occurrences, all collaborators see the skip.

#### 2.3 Group Timelines
A shared space where a group of people all contribute and view events.

**Examples:**
- **Family timeline:** Mom creates "Smith Family" group. Adds all birthdays, holidays, kids' school dates, family trips. Invites spouse and adult kids. Everyone sees the same timeline. Anyone can add events (subject to admin approval).
- **Friend group:** "The Squad" group. Everyone adds events they want the group to know about — concerts, trips, birthdays.
- **Team timeline:** Work-focused (see Team layer).

**Privacy model:**
- Public group: anyone with link can view
- Private group: invite-only, view only
- Member group: invite-only, members can contribute

**Event visibility:** Group members can mark events "I'm attending" or "I'm interested." Shows social proof on each event ("8 of 12 members are going").

#### 2.4 Embeddable Countdown Widget
A JavaScript snippet that embeds a live countdown on any webpage.

```html
<script src="https://cdn.daysuntil.app/widget.js"
  data-event-id="evt_abc123"
  data-theme="dark"
  data-size="large">
</script>
```

**Widget sizes:** Nano (just number), Small (number + label), Medium (full card), Large (full countdown with subscribe button)

**Themes:** Light, Dark, Glass, Custom (hex colors)

**Use cases:**
- Wedding website: "Our big day is in X days"
- Event landing page: "Conference opens in X days — early bird ends sooner"
- Shopify store: "Sale ends in X hours"
- Personal portfolio: "Available for hire in X days" (notice period countdown)
- Gym/studio: "Next class starts in X minutes"
- Kickstarter: "Campaign ends in X days"

**Pricing for widgets:** Free for personal pages. $9/mo for commercial use (removes Days Until branding). White-label available on Enterprise.

#### 2.5 Reactions & Social Feed
On shared countdown pages and group timelines:

- Emoji reactions on events
- Comments / hype messages as event approaches
- Day-of celebration thread ("🎉 We made it!")
- Post-event reflection: "How was it?" prompt sent to all subscribers after the event
- Photo uploads post-event ("Add memories from [event]")

**The post-event lifecycle is a key retention mechanic.** Most calendar apps end at the event. Days Until continues the relationship — the event becomes a memory, and the memory links to future anniversaries.

#### 2.6 Social Moments Feed
An optional discovery feed showing public countdowns from your network and trending public events.

- See what your friends are counting down to
- Browse trending public countdowns (major concerts, sports events, movie releases)
- "Join countdown" to add a public event to your personal timeline with one tap

**Curated public events:** Days Until maintains a verified library of major cultural events — World Cup games, Oscar nominations, product launches, holidays. Users can subscribe with one tap.

#### 3.1 Team Workspaces
A shared environment for a company, team, or project.

**Workspace structure:**
```
Workspace: Acme Corp
├── All Hands
│   ├── Q2 Board Meeting (June 15)
│   ├── All-hands presentation (June 14)
│   └── Q2 metrics locked (June 13)

│   └── App Store submission (June 27)
└── People Ops
    ├── Q2 performance reviews (June 30)
    ├── Benefits open enrollment (June 1-14)
    └── Summer intern start (June 3)
```

**Permissions model:**
- Workspace admin: full control
- Team manager: manage their team's events
- Member: view all, add to their team
- Viewer: read-only (for executives, stakeholders, investors)

**Event ownership:** Each event has an owner and optional assignees. Owner is responsible for it being done on time.

#### 3.2 Deadline Health Scoring
The AI continuously assesses each team event for risk.

**Health score factors:**
- Days remaining vs. complexity of event type
- Number of open "blockers" (sub-tasks marked as blocking)
- Historical team performance on similar deadlines
- Recent activity (last update to the event)
- Assignee's current workload (number of open events in near-term)

**Health states:**
- 🟢 **On Track** — sufficient runway, clear owner, active progress
- 🟡 **At Risk** — less runway than expected for complexity, some blockers
- 🔴 **Critical** — insufficient runway, multiple blockers, or stale
- ⚫ **Missed** — past due

**Daily digest to team lead:** "3 events in your workspace are At Risk. [Event A]: 8 days remaining, 2 blockers. [Event B]: 5 days remaining, no recent activity. [Event C]: owner hasn't logged in in 4 days."

#### 3.3 Blockers & Dependencies
Each team event can have:
- **Sub-tasks / checklist items** — granular steps to completion
- **Blockers** — explicit "this event cannot complete until [other event] is done"
- **Dependencies** — "this event starts after [other event] completes"

**Critical path visualization:** In the team timeline view, a Gantt-style critical path shows which events block others. Red highlighting on critical path events that are at risk.

#### 3.4 Integrations
**Linear:** Pull in milestones and cycle end dates. When a Linear milestone is marked done, the Days Until event automatically closes.

**GitHub:** Pull in release dates and milestone deadlines. When a GitHub release is published, mark the corresponding event complete.

**Jira:** Same as Linear/GitHub for enterprise clients.

**Notion:** Two-way sync with Notion database dates. A date property in Notion creates/updates a Days Until event.

**Slack:** 
- `/daysuntil` slash command: `@daysuntil what's coming up this week?` → bot responds with team events in the next 7 days
- Automated standups: every morning at 9am, bot posts "Good morning team. Here's what's due this week: [list]"
- Milestone celebrations: when an event passes, bot posts "🎉 [Product team] just shipped iOS 3.0. That's a wrap!"
- At-risk alerts: when an event goes 🔴 Critical, bot pings the owner in DM

**Google Calendar / Outlook:** Two-way sync. Team events appear in calendar. Calendar events of certain types can be pulled into Days Until.

**Zapier / Make:** Trigger any workflow from Days Until events. "When an event reaches T-7, send a Slack message to #marketing." "When an event completes, create a Confluence retrospective page."

#### 3.5 Retrospectives & Post-Mortems
After every major team event completes (or is missed), Days Until prompts for a retrospective.

**Auto-generated retrospective template:**
```
## iOS 3.0 Launch — Retrospective
Target date: July 1, 2026
Actual date: July 3, 2026 (2 days late)

Blockers that caused delay:
- App Store review took 4 days (expected 2)
- Beta feedback required 2 unplanned bug fixes

What went well:
[team fills in]

What to improve:
[team fills in]

For next launch: add 5 buffer days for App Store review
```

**AI-powered analysis:** After 3+ retrospectives, AI starts surfacing patterns: "Your team consistently underestimates App Store review time. Consider adding a 5-day buffer to all future App Store submissions."

#### 3.6 Investor / Board Timeline
A special read-only view designed for investors and board members.

**What it shows:**
- Upcoming milestones (product launches, fundraising deadlines, hiring goals)
- Recent completions (what shipped on time vs. late)
- Team health overview (how many events on track vs. at risk)
- Runway countdown (days until cash out)
- KPI targets with deadlines

**Investor portal link:** Share a unique read-only URL with each investor. They can view your timeline anytime without needing a login.

**Fundraising mode:** When a startup is in a fundraising round, a special view shows: "Round closes in X days. $Y committed of $Z target."

### Layer 4: Developer Platform

#### 4.1 Public API
Full REST API with comprehensive documentation.

**Endpoints:**
```
GET    /events              — list all events
POST   /events              — create event
GET    /events/{id}         — get event
PATCH  /events/{id}         — update event
DELETE /events/{id}         — delete event
GET    /events/{id}/checklist — get AI prep checklist
POST   /events/{id}/complete  — mark event complete
GET    /users/me            — current user profile
GET    /groups/{id}/events  — list group events
POST   /webhooks            — create webhook
GET    /webhooks            — list webhooks
DELETE /webhooks/{id}       — delete webhook
```

**Authentication:** API keys (Bearer token). Per-key rate limits and scopes.

**Rate limits:**

## 6. AI Strategy

### Model Architecture

**Event Classification:** Fine-tuned small model (likely Llama-3.1-8B or similar) for event type classification. Runs at creation time, cost ~$0.0001 per event. Could run on-device eventually.

**Prep Checklist Generation:** Claude Sonnet 3.7 or GPT-4o. Expensive but runs once per event and is cached forever. Estimated cost: $0.002 per checklist. At 1M events with checklists: $2,000/month — negligible vs. revenue.

**Reminder Intelligence:** Rules-based engine with ML override. Start with a decision tree (event type + days remaining → reminder timing). Layer on ML personalization after 90 days of user data.

**Pattern Insights:** Time-series analysis on user event data. Can be done with lightweight models (Prophet, simple regression). No need for large LLMs.

**Natural Language Quick Add:** Current Groq/Llama3 setup. Will stay serverless. Add support for: recurring event creation ("remind me every Monday to review my finances"), multi-event creation ("add my three deadlines: A on June 1, B on June 15, C on July 1"), and context-aware suggestions ("I notice you fly a lot — want me to add a 'check-in opens' reminder for your upcoming flight?").

### AI Personalization Flywheel

```
User adds events
    ↓
AI classifies + generates checklist
    ↓
User interacts with reminders (dismisses, acts on, snoozes)
    ↓
AI learns optimal reminder timing for this user
    ↓
User adds more events because the product is more useful
    ↓
More data → better personalization → harder to leave
```

This flywheel is the core moat. After 6 months of use, Days Until knows your temporal patterns better than any other tool. Switching cost becomes very high.

### Privacy-First AI

All personal pattern analysis is:
- Processed in isolated per-user contexts
- Never used for advertising targeting
- Never sold to third parties
- Exportable (full data export in JSON)
- Deletable (full account deletion removes all ML training data)

This is a feature, not a constraint. The trust this builds with users is a competitive advantage, especially against Google/Apple.

## 10. Competitive Landscape

### Direct Competitors

**Countdown apps (iOS/Android):** Days Until (ironically, there's an app with this exact name), Countdown+ Star, Big Days.
- **Their weakness:** Toy apps. No AI, no social, no team features, no web app, business model is one-time purchase ($2-5).
- **Our advantage:** Full platform vs. single utility. They can't catch up without rebuilding from scratch.

**Google Calendar:**
- **Their weakness:** Logistical, not emotional. Every event is equal. No preparation intelligence. No social countdown pages. No countdown display. No enterprise deadline health.
- **Our advantage:** We're complementary, not competitive. Most users will use both. We're the emotional layer; they're the scheduling layer. We should integrate *with* them.
- **Risk:** Google builds this. Mitigation: they've had 20 years to add countdown features and haven't. They're focused on scheduling, not time awareness. And if Google copies us, it validates the category.

**Notion:**
- **Their weakness:** Static. You can write down upcoming dates, but Notion doesn't push you, doesn't help you prepare, doesn't count down. Database views can show dates, but there's no urgency.
- **Our advantage:** We integrate with Notion. Notion users who want their dates to feel alive use Days Until.

**Linear:**
- **Their weakness:** Developer-focused issue tracking. No personal time layer. No emotional events. No social features.
- **Our advantage:** Linear is for tasks; we're for milestones. We integrate with Linear and serve the gaps they explicitly don't want to fill (personal events, external deadlines, social countdowns).

**Asana / Monday.com:**
- **Their weakness:** Heavyweight project management. Complex setup. No personal layer. No countdown UX.
- **Our advantage:** Lightweight, fast, personal-first. We catch users who find Asana overwhelming.

### Indirect Competitors

**Apple Reminders / Google Tasks:** Too simple. No team features, no AI, no social.

**Todoist / TickTick:** Task-focused. No countdown UX, no social, limited team features.

**Calendly:** Owns scheduling; we own countdown and preparation. Natural integration partners, not competitors.

### Our Defensible Position

We win by being **the only product that treats time as emotional, not just logistical**. Our moats:

1. **AI personalization data** — after 6 months, our model knows your temporal patterns. Nobody else has this data or can build it without years of usage.
2. **Network effects** — shared countdowns, group timelines, team workspaces all get more valuable as more people join.
3. **Ecosystem lock-in** — widgets embedded on 50,000 websites, Zapier integrations in 100,000 workflows — these don't switch easily.
4. **Brand** — "Days Until" becomes the verb for temporal anticipation, the way "Google" became the verb for searching.

- Move fast — be so embedded in users' lives before big tech moves that switching cost is enormous
- The social and team layers are inherently harder for big tech to build (distribution problems)
- Google has had 20 years to build countdown features into Calendar. They're focused on scheduling, not emotion.
- If acquired: great outcome.

### Risk 2: Poor AI checklist quality kills trust
**Probability:** Medium. **Impact:** Medium.

**Mitigation:**
- Launch checklists in beta, explicitly labeled "AI-generated — edit freely"
- Build feedback mechanism from day 1 (thumbs up/down per item)
- Use feedback to continuously improve prompts
- Set low initial expectations; overdeliver rather than underdeliver

### Risk 3: Consumer churn — events are finite
**Probability:** Medium. **Impact:** Medium.

**The risk:** Users add events, they pass, and then the user has fewer events and less reason to open the app.

**Mitigation:**
- Recurring events keep the app always relevant
- Pattern insights give the app value even when event list is sparse
- Post-event memory collection creates a reason to come back
- Annual anniversary reminders ("1 year ago today was your wedding — here's what you wrote")
- Onboarding that helps users add 10+ events across multiple life areas immediately

### Risk 4: B2B sales is expensive and slow
**Probability:** High. **Impact:** Low (in early stages — avoid this until consumer is working).

**Mitigation:**
- Don't build enterprise features until team features are validated
- Let enterprise come inbound first — sign them as design partners
- Only hire sales reps when there's a repeatable sales motion

### Risk 5: Notification fatigue kills engagement
**Probability:** Medium. **Impact:** High.

**Mitigation:**
- Default to minimal notifications — users opt in to more
- Intelligent batching — never more than 1 notification per day per user
- Easy notification preferences (type, frequency, channel)
- Study Duolingo's playbook on notifications that feel good, not annoying

### Risk 6: Copycat from a better-funded competitor
**Probability:** Low. **Impact:** High if it's a large incumbent.

**Mitigation:**
- Speed of execution is the only real defense early
- Build the AI personalization flywheel aggressively — it's very hard to replicate without years of user data
- Patents on novel AI interaction patterns (prep checklist generation + reminder cascading)
- Community and brand are moats that money can't instantly buy
